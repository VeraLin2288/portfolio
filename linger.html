Depressed Lingering Traveler

We are all lingering travelers. Grow up, leave hometown, move on and integrate into the new environment. It is a circle that no one is able to escape. Humans are gregarious animals, eager for a sense of belonging and security in groups. I am an international student and it has been two years I could not go back to distant hometown. In the mood of homesickness, the short days and foul weather conspire to drive me into the ground.


However, it is a common phenomenon to suffer from seasonal depression in such an environment. It often makes me feel empty and sad, lack of energy and oversleeping. So for the first thematic work, I decided to take photography on surrounding things from the perspective of people with seasonal depression patients represented by me. The purpose of this project is to explore the feeling and emotion of seasonal depression. And it also means to study how the environment triggers seasonal depression. To a certain extent, the color of photo is connected with emotion. The cold-tone photos express my depression, homesickness and loneliness to viewers in the most direct way. When choosing the shooting location and subject, I chose the scene of snow and sunset which are most easy to trigger people's sadness and depression.


I hope when the warm spring comes, seasonal depression will leave me. But these photos record my emotion and feeling when I was a lingering traveller in a foreign country. After few years I will leave here, move on and the photos recall the memory when I look back.
