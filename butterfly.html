Girl & Butterfly

I created a collage and animation to challenge the anti-gender ideology movement, telling the story of a girl struggling in a dark, oppressive environment. She experiences sorrow, stress, and fragility, represented by her fading transparency and the bloody red glass fragments piercing her—symbols of harsh judgment and societal harm. Vines wrapping around her arms signify constraints, but as they break away, she regains strength, growing larger and more transparent, symbolizing her courage to fight for freedom and rights. The church bell at the end evokes hope and persistence, while butterflies represent her final sense of happiness and liberation. The animation is divided into two contrasting parts, reflecting a woman’s awakening self-awareness—transforming from weak and constrained to strong and independent, breaking free from traditional gender roles. Inspired by Judith Butler’s speech “Who is Afraid of Gender?”, I highlight how the anti-gender ideology movement enforces rigid gender frameworks, threatening women's rights, LGBTQ+ equality, and societal progress. The girl kneeling behind flowers symbolizes women pleading for protection from patriarchal oppression. This project expresses my resistance against societal and traditional constraints on women, advocating for gender equality, self-liberation, and the right to define one’s identity freely.
